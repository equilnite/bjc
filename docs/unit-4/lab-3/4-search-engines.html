    <div class="forYouToDo" id="first">
        <ol>
            <li>Open the search engine you usually use, the <a href="https://duckduckgo.com/" target="_blank" title="duckduckgo.com">DuckDuckGo</a> search engine, and another search engine such as <a href="http://google.com" target="_blank" title="google.com">Google</a>, <a href="http://yahoo.com" target="_blank" title="yahoo.com">Yahoo</a>, or <a href="http://bing.com" target="_blank" title="bing.com">Bing</a>. Search for a phrase, like "Internet search engines" or "New York restaurants." Do you get the same search results at each site? Try a search related to politics, such as "Barack Obama."</li>
            <li>Discuss with a partner, then share with the class. How do you think a search engine works? What algorithm might the search engine use to decide what to show you?</li>
            <li>Search results are different in different countries, even from the same search engine. Investigate the reasons for this. (To start, do a web search for &quot;search censorship.&quot;)</li>
            <li>In <a href="http://www.bitsbook.com/wp-content/uploads/2008/12/chapter4.pdf" target="_blank" title="Blown to Bits"><em>Blown to Bits</em></a>, the authors claim that "search is a new form of control over information" (p. 111) and "search is power" (p. 145). Why might it be important to think of the social implications behind searching on the Internet?</li>
        </ol>
    </div>
            
    <div class="takeItFurther">
        <ol type="A">
            <li>Google keeps track of searches. You can check out <a href="http://www.google.com/trends/hottrends" target="_blank" title="Google Trends">the day's trending topics</a> or <a href="http://www.google.com/trends/topcharts?hl=en#vm=cat&geo=US&date=2014&cid" target="_blank" title="Google Trends 2014">the top searches of 2014 (or any year back to 2004) in the United States (or globally)</a>. What are benefits and costs to society in general from this tracking? What are benefits and costs to individual users from this tracking?</li>
        </ol>
